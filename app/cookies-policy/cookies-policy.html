1. What Are Cookies and Similar Technologies?

In short: They are small tools used by browsers and apps to remember information and help services work properly.

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. Similar technologies, such as local storage, session storage, and analytics identifiers, can also store or remember information in your browser or device.

These technologies can help a platform recognize a session, remember settings, understand usage patterns, improve performance, and support security features.

2. How We Use Them

In short: We use these technologies to run Spreadz, keep it secure, remember preferences, and understand how the product is being used.

Spreadz uses cookies and similar technologies to:

  • Keep the platform working properly.
  • Remember basic preferences and product state.
  • Support authentication, security, and abuse prevention.
  • Measure usage, diagnose issues, and improve performance and reliability.

We do not use cookies to personally track you across unrelated websites for our own advertising purposes.

3. Types of Cookies and Technologies We Use

a) Essential technologies

These are required for core product functionality, reliability, and security. Without them, parts of Spreadz may not function correctly.

  • Basic platform functionality.
  • Security and fraud prevention.
  • Remembering limited session or device state needed for the app to operate.

b) Authentication and session technologies

We use Supabase and related session/authentication tools to support access, persistence, and account/session handling. Depending on the part of the product, this may involve cookies, browser storage, or similar session technologies.

  • Identifying and maintaining a session.
  • Keeping authorized users signed in where applicable.
  • Reducing unauthorized access and account misuse.

c) Analytics and performance technologies

We use analytics and measurement tools such as Google Analytics and, where enabled, PostHog to understand how users interact with Spreadz and how the product is performing.

  • Page views and general usage patterns.
  • Performance and reliability data.
  • Product interaction data and diagnostics.
  • Session replay or recording tools, where enabled, to help debug and improve the experience.

This information is typically used in aggregate or pseudonymous form, but it may still involve browser or device identifiers.

d) Preference and local device storage

Spreadz stores some information locally in your browser or device to remember settings and improve usability.

  • Profile setup state.
  • Saved interests or product preferences.
  • Notification-related state and similar app settings.

4. Third-Party Services

In short: Some third-party services used inside Spreadz may place their own cookies or use similar technologies.

Spreadz may rely on third-party services that use their own cookies or tracking technologies, subject to their own policies. Examples may include:

  • Google Analytics for traffic and usage measurement.
  • PostHog for product analytics and, where enabled, session recording.
  • GIPHY or similar media providers for GIF and rich media functionality.
  • Supabase and related infrastructure services for session and platform functionality.

We do not control how third-party providers use their own cookies or similar technologies once their services are loaded through the platform.

5. Your Choices

In short: You can control cookies through your browser and device settings, but some features may stop working.

You can usually:

  • Disable or block cookies through your browser settings.
  • Clear cookies and local storage at any time.
  • Adjust certain analytics or privacy settings through your browser, device, or relevant third-party tools where available.

If you disable certain cookies or similar technologies, parts of Spreadz may not work correctly, including sign-in behavior, saved preferences, media loading, and some security-related functionality.

We may update this Cookies Policy from time to time as the platform, our providers, or our legal obligations evolve. When we do, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page.
