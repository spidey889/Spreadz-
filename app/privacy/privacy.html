1. What Information Do We Collect?

Information you provide directly

In short: We collect information you choose to give us when you set up a profile or use chat features.

Depending on how you use Spreadz, we may collect information such as:

  • Your display name, generated or chosen username, and college or university name.
  • Your profile photo or avatar image if you upload one.
  • Chat messages, GIFs, room interactions, friend requests, and other content you submit through the Services.
  • Reports or moderation-related submissions if you flag another user or message.
  • Administrative inputs if you use restricted admin tools.

You are responsible for making sure the information you provide is accurate and appropriate to share.

Information collected automatically

In short: We collect technical and usage information automatically when you use the app.

We automatically collect certain device and usage information to help operate, secure, and improve Spreadz. This may include your IP address, browser type, device characteristics, operating system, language settings, referral information, general location derived from technical signals, and how you interact with rooms and features.

We also collect analytics and diagnostics data, including product-usage events and, where enabled through our analytics tooling, session-level behavioral data that helps us debug and improve the experience.

Information stored on your device

Spreadz stores some data locally in your browser or device so the app can function properly. This may include your generated identifiers, saved interests, profile setup state, notification prompt status, local friend list cache, and similar product preferences.

2. How Do We Use Your Information?

In short: We use information to run the app, personalize chat experiences, improve safety, and support notifications and analytics.

We may use your information to:

  • Create and maintain your profile and app session.
  • Show your display name, avatar, college, and messages inside the chat experience.
  • Personalize room ordering or recommendations based on prior usage and saved interests.
  • Operate friend requests, reports, moderation workflows, and abuse prevention measures.
  • Enable optional push notifications and related delivery systems.
  • Debug issues, monitor service health, measure usage, and improve product quality.
  • Comply with legal obligations and enforce our Terms of Service.

3. When and With Whom Do We Share Information?

In short: We share information with infrastructure and analytics providers, with other users where the product requires it, and when legally necessary.

We may share information in the following situations:

  • With infrastructure providers. We use third-party services to host databases, file storage, and related backend infrastructure.
  • With analytics providers. We may use product analytics tools to understand feature usage, diagnose issues, and improve the experience.
  • With notification and browser platforms. If you enable push notifications, relevant subscription and delivery data may be processed through browser or notification systems.
  • With other users of Spreadz. Your display name, avatar, college, and messages may be visible to other users when you participate in chat rooms or profile-related interactions.
  • For moderation and safety. We may review or share relevant content internally to investigate reports, abuse, fraud, or misuse of the Services.
  • For legal reasons. We may disclose information if required by law, legal process, or a valid government request, or to protect the rights, safety, and integrity of Spreadz or others.
  • Business transfers. If Spreadz is involved in a merger, acquisition, financing, or asset sale, relevant information may be transferred as part of that transaction.

4. Cookies, Local Storage, and Analytics

In short: We use browser storage and analytics tools to remember settings, keep the app working, and learn how people use it.

Spreadz uses local storage and similar browser technologies to remember product state and preferences. This can include user identifiers stored on-device, interests you selected, prompt dismissals, notification state, and cached UI data.

We may also use analytics tools to collect product events, performance data, and diagnostic information. These tools may use cookies, browser storage, or comparable technical identifiers.

If you block or clear these technologies, some parts of the app may stop working properly or lose saved preferences.

5. Push Notifications

In short: Push notifications are optional and require your permission.

If you choose to enable notifications, we may store a browser push subscription and related technical details so we can send message alerts or other product notifications to your device.

You can disable notifications in your browser, device, or app settings at any time. If a push subscription expires or becomes invalid, we may remove it from our systems.

By using the Services, you understand that your information may be transferred to and processed in places that may have different data protection laws than your home jurisdiction.
